The first moment in limbo where I experienced something strong in relation to the reading was upon seeing the boy himself.

I feel like this resonated with me after learning of Lacan's Mirror, as it talks about "being reflected by another's image".

One of the reasons I feel like this is because when we first start the game, we have no backstory or anything.

The boy simply wakes up and that is the start. I believe that this can also be said for the boy, as he may not remember anything before waking up.

.

Another example of Lacan's mirror in effect here is when we first learn the controls of the game.

There is no tutorial or anything to go by so we are having to learn the controls from scratch, which can likely also be said for the boy.

We then have to venture through the forest without any knowledge of what awaits us, similar to how the boy may feel, being ignorant to what lies ahead.

Image of the boy stumbling across a hung corpse

This image here shows the boy coming across a handing body.

The boy may find himself relating to the corpse, since Limbo is a word used to describe a sort of purgatory after death, it can be inferred that the boy is already dead.

The boy may therefore be stumbling across his own corpse.

Quote from Lacan: The Mirror Stage overview: "The infant identifies with the image, which serves as a gestalt of the infant's emerging perceptions of selfhood"

What this means is the boy could identify with the deceased body, and believe that it is symbolic of what happened to him.

However, connecting with the idea that Limbo is simply the boy going through a purgatory, this could also mean that the boy is stumbling across his own body in the afterlife.
